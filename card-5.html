Break the Trail, Post After Leaving

Home Quick Rules
Minimisation Confidentiality Best Interests

Vignette

Daily short reels show morning routines, therapy pickup, school drop-offs, and bedtime. A local follower pieces together routes and approaches the family in public.

Forces & Risks

  • Authenticity & community vs. physical safety & privacy
  • Consistency for growth vs. revealing predictable patterns
  • Location tags & timestamps vs. minimised footprint

Risk path: traceability → intrusion/stalking → safety & emotional harm
Contextual Integrity: home/school routines leaking to broad local audiences

Prompts

Rights: Ask first—routines can feel invasive to children and peers.

Risk: Repeated posts create a location trail. Time-shift or narrow the audience?

Norms: Many parents post later (after leaving) and avoid school/hospital tags.

Assent

Helpers

  • Time-shift posts (after leaving).
  • Avoid location tags and crop street signs/school logos.
  • Non-identifying thumbnail (no house numbers, uniforms).
  • Save to draft and ask when the child is present.
  • Time-shift and strip timestamps.
  • Remix Off • Downloads Off • Location Off
  • Close Friends ; time-shift and avoid school tags.
  • Trim route cues (bus stops, recurring storefronts).
  • Remix/Downloads/Location Off
  • Private/Close Friends ; keep as draft unless assent changes.
  • Remove the trail —reduce frequency, randomise timing.
  • Comments Off • Remix Off • Downloads Off • Location Off
Bystanders & co-ownership: blur classmates and school staff; remove badges/uniforms.

Law & Policy

“Children have a right to be heard.” — UNCRC GC No. 12 “High privacy by default.” — ICO Children's Code Assent in pediatrics — AAP (2016) / AAP (1995) COPPA scope — operators collecting from <13s, not parents posting: FTC COPPA France (2024) — children's image rights law (platform duties & parental duties): overview

Even without GPS tags, repeating places/times can reveal routes. Time-shift and narrow audience to reduce traceability.

Evidence hooks

  • Minkus et al., WWW 2015: Parents' posts can compromise children's privacy via identifiable patterns and metadata.
  • Schirmer et al., 2024: Visibility signals (thumbnails, aesthetics) correlate with exposure; repeated cues aid inference.
  • Stephenson et al., 2024: Local followers + parasocial ties increase offline approach risk; time-shift reduces real-time traceability.
