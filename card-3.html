Vignette

A therapist (or parent) wants to post short clips from a child's therapy session to show progress or demonstrate techniques. The footage reveals the child's condition and the clinic context; other patients or staff may appear in the background.

Forces & Risks

  • Education/advocacy vs. confidentiality & dignity
  • Progress evidence vs. long-lived trace of diagnosis
  • Community praise vs. bullying or stigma later

Risk path: disclosure → identifiability → reputational/emotional harm
Contextual Integrity: clinical context leaking into broad, remixable audiences

Prompts

Rights: This is clinic footage—ask the child first and respect dissent.

Risk: Clinical details spread quickly. Prefer private sharing or Close Friends.

Norms: Many clinicians/parents share technique notes without faces or clinic signs.

Assent

Helpers

  • Blur/crop clinic markers (logos, wall boards, badges).
  • Check bystanders ; blur other patients/parents.
  • Non-identifying thumbnail
  • Save to draft and ask when the child is present.
  • Comments: Limited/Off
  • Remix Off • Downloads Off • Location Off
  • Close Friends / Private
  • Share technique, not faces (hands/tools, text overlays).
  • Comments: Limited/Off ; Remix/Downloads/Location Off.
  • Private only ; keep as draft unless assent changes.
  • Comments Off • Remix Off • Downloads Off • Location Off
  • Remove clinic identifiers (boards, signage, badges).
Bystanders & co-ownership: If peers/families appear, obtain consent/assent or blur/crop them out.

Law & Policy

“Children have a right to be heard.” — UNCRC GC No. 12 “High privacy by default.” — ICO Children's Code Assent in pediatrics — AAP (2016) / AAP (1995)

HIPAA typically governs providers, not parents—but clinic contexts still warrant high-privacy defaults and minimisation.

Evidence hooks

  • AAP, 2016: Assent is not the same as absence of refusal; developmentally appropriate “yes” should guide sharing; dissent respected where possible.
  • Van Goidsenhoven & De Schauwer, 2022: Consent/assent are relational and ongoing—especially for complex communication needs.
  • Karmiris, 2024: Ethical conundrums in assent for intellectual disabilities; create multiple modalities for comfort/discomfort.
  • Hays & Butauski, 2018: Families weigh disclosure vs stigma; clinical visuals increase identifiability and local recognition.
