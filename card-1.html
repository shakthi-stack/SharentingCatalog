Ask First, Tighten by Default

Scenario

Parent is about to post a short of their child's meltdown (#autismmom) to seek advice. Comments, remix, and downloads can make the clip broadly searchable and reusable; in NDD contexts, stigma and identifiability risks are higher.

Why this matters (Risks and tradeoffs)

  • Support vs dignity & discoverability
  • Speed to post vs consulting the child
  • Platform virality/remix

Risk path: exposure → increased accessibility → secondary use
Contextual Integrity: family-support content leaking to broad, remixable audiences

Ask and Decide

Rights: Children have a say. Ask first, or share to a smaller circle.

Risk: Sensitive moment. Reduce copying/remix, or save to ask first?

Norms: Parents in similar situations often choose Close Friends.

Assent Check

How to do it (Quick tips)

  • Status only Review current settings; proceed if Assent was clearly given.
  • Non-identifying thumbnail
  • Save to draft and ask when the child is present.
  • Remix Off • Downloads Off • Location Off
  • Blur/crop faces or sensitive text.
  • Close Friends
  • Remix Off • Downloads Off • Location Off
  • Non-identifying thumbnail; blur peers in background.
  • Private/Close Friends ; keep as draft unless Assent changes.
  • Remix Off • Downloads Off • Location Off
  • Manage associated remixes; use a non-identifying thumbnail.
Bystanders & co-ownership: Check peers/family faces; blur if Assent is unknown.

Law & Policy

"Children have a right to be heard" UNCRC GC No. 12 "High privacy by default" ICO Children's Code France (2024) children's image rights law (platform duties & parental duties): overview Assent in pediatrics AAP (2016 update) / AAP (1995)

Why is it recommended (evidence)

  • Stephenson et al., CHI 2024: TikTok's remix/virality can "amplify both benefits and risks," intensifying exposure and reuse of sensitive clips.
  • Amon et al., PACM HCI 2022: Parents commonly post without asking; children "want to be asked more often" (parent-child permission gap).
  • Moser et al., CHI 2017: Children report discomfort/embarrassment when parents share without consultation; asking first improves perceived fairness.
  • Solove, 2006: Risk path framing via "exposure → increased accessibility → secondary use/appropriation."
  • Walrave et al., 2023: "Mindful sharenting" = balancing community support with protection; Ask and Decide that bind to concrete controls help.
